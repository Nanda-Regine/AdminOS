- Your billing and payment records

- Their own performance review records
- Their own training and qualification records

### Records Subject to Possible Refusal
The following categories of records may be refused on applicable PAIA grounds:
- Source code and technical architecture (trade secrets — Section 67)
- Records revealing commercial information of other tenants (Section 68)
- Records that would harm Mirembe Muse's commercial interests (Section 68)
- Legally privileged communications (Section 67)
- Records relating to third parties where disclosure would constitute a POPIA breach (Section 71)

## SECTION 8 — HOW TO SUBMIT A PAIA REQUEST

### Step 1 — Complete the Request Form
Use the prescribed Form C (PAIA Regulations, Government Gazette 31688):
- Download from: www.justice.gov.za/inforeg/
- Or request a copy from the Information Officer

### Step 2 — Include in Your Request
- Your full name and contact details
- A description of the record(s) you require
- Your preferred form of access (copy, inspection, summary)
- If requesting on behalf of someone else: proof of authority
- If you are a data subject requesting your own personal information: proof of identity

### Step 3 — Submit the Request
**Email:** [YOUR EMAIL]

## SECTION 10 — GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL

Mirembe Muse (Pty) Ltd may refuse access to records on the following grounds (PAIA Chapter 4):

- **Section 62:** Mandatory protection of privacy of third parties (personal information)
- **Section 63:** Mandatory protection of commercial information of third parties
- **Section 64:** Mandatory protection of certain confidential information of third parties
- **Section 65:** Mandatory protection of safety of individuals and protection of property
- **Section 66:** Mandatory protection of police dockets in bail proceedings
- **Section 67:** Protection of research information
- **Section 68:** Commercial information of the private body (trade secrets, financial information)
- **Section 69:** Confidential correspondence of the private body
- **Section 70:** Information that would prejudice a protected third party
- **Section 71:** Information obtained in confidence

Where a record contains both refusable and non-refusable information, the refusable portions will be severed and the remainder provided (Section 28).

## SECTION 11 — REMEDIES IF REQUEST IS REFUSED

If your PAIA request is refused, you may:

1. **Request internal appeal** — Write to the Information Officer within 60 days requesting reconsideration, providing grounds for why the refusal is incorrect

2. **Approach the Information Regulator** — File a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa:
   - Email: inforeg@justice.gov.za
   - Tel: 010 023 5200
   - Address: JD House, 27 Stiemens Street, Braamfontein, Johannesburg, 2001

3. **Apply to High Court** — Apply to the relevant High Court for an order compelling disclosure (Section 82 of PAIA)

## SECTION 12 — SUBJECT ACCESS REQUESTS UNDER POPIA

In addition to PAIA rights, data subjects whose personal information is processed by Mirembe Muse (Pty) Ltd have the following rights under POPIA:

| Right | How to Exercise |
|---|---|
| **Access** — know what personal information we hold about you | Email the Information Officer |
| **Correction** — correct inaccurate personal information | Email the Information Officer or update your AdminOS profile |
| **Deletion** — request deletion of your personal information (right to erasure) | Use the in-app POPIA erasure tool in AdminOS settings, or email the Information Officer |
| **Objection** — object to processing of your personal information | Email the Information Officer with reasons |
| **Restriction** — restrict processing in certain circumstances | Email the Information Officer |
| **Complaint** — lodge a complaint about how we handle your data | First to us (IO), then to the Information Regulator |

**Response time for POPIA requests:** We will respond within 30 days.
**No fee** is charged for POPIA subject access requests.
