## The problem

Most small and micro businesses in South Africa run on a folder, a spreadsheet, a WhatsApp thread, and three half-used apps — plus everything the owner is holding in their head. There's no back office. The plumber, the caterer, the salon owner, the township retailer, the small services firm: they're too small for a SAP/Xero enterprise stack and a team of specialists, but too real to keep running on memory.

The result is the same story everywhere:

- Money is **earned but never collected** — unpaid invoices quietly starve the business.
- **Payroll and compliance** are a source of dread (PAYE/UIF/SDL, VAT, licences, safety, the year-end scramble with the accountant).
- Customer conversations live in **WhatsApp chaos** with no record.
- The owner **can't afford** a bookkeeper, a debt collector, an HR manager, a copywriter, and a business analyst — so they do all of it, badly, at night.

## What AdminOS is

A **multi-tenant business operating system** that pulls the whole back office into one place and puts AI agents to work on the admin. It's not "an app with pages" — it's organised as a **proactive operating loop**: six persona-led cockpits (Money, Ops, Sales, People, Governance) plus a Command Center that each **lead with the single most important thing to do next**, so you manage by exception instead of digging.

Under the hood it's a real system of record — tenant-isolated data, row-level security, a genuine payroll engine, automated workflows — not a demo.

## The pain points it solves

### 1. "My admin is scattered across eight tools and my head."
Invoicing, payroll, staff, contracts, bookings, inventory, expenses, documents and compliance dates — **organised in one place**, all linked to the same customer and staff records. One workspace instead of a folder, a spreadsheet, a chat thread, and three apps.

### 2. "I've earned the money but haven't collected it."
The **Chase** debt-recovery engine tracks who owes you and sends escalating, professional reminders automatically — from a friendly nudge to a letter of demand — with an owner-controlled autonomy setting so it never sends a legal threat you didn't sanction. The Money cockpit opens with *"you're owed R X — chase the oldest first."*

### 3. "Payroll and compliance terrify me."
A real **payroll engine** computes PAYE, UIF and SDL and generates payslips. A pre-seeded **SA compliance calendar** tracks licences, health & safety incidents, employment-equity data and contract-expiry dates. And an **accountant-ready monthly reports pack** (VAT201 working paper, general journal, income statement — all CSV) turns the year-end handoff into a download instead of a crisis.

### 4. "I can't afford a team of specialists."
Instead of hiring, you get **six AI agents plus a mentor** (see below) that answer customers, chase invoices, draft your emails, read your documents, brief you every morning, and check in on your team.

### 5. "Customers reach me on WhatsApp and it's chaos."
A unified **Inbox** for every inbound conversation, an **online booking engine**, and a magic-link **Client Portal** where clients view invoices, pay online, and submit documents — no password to remember.

### 6. "I'm informal and don't know how to formalise or grow."
A **Business Academy** with a personal AI mentor, **stokvel** (community savings) tooling, peer mentorship, a formalisation pathway, B-BBEE data capture, a **business-health score**, a **90-day cash-flow forecast**, and a **business-valuation model** — the tools to move from informal survival toward a formal, fundable business.

## The idea that ties it together

AdminOS is a **proactive OS with a loop**, not a filing cabinet. Each cockpit reads the state of your business and opens with the one action that matters most right now — *"3 bookings need confirming," "cash is healthy — keep invoicing," "a licence expires in 14 days."* A brand-new business is greeted with setup nudges, not empty screens. The goal is simple: **you should always know the next right move without going looking for it.**
